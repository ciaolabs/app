## What the Personality Survey Measures

The personality assessment measures stable patterns in how you think, feel, and behave. It uses multiple established frameworks to capture different facets of your personality, giving you a multi-dimensional picture rather than a single label.

Scores reflect your typical tendencies — how you usually are, not how you behave in every situation. Personality traits are relatively stable across time but can shift gradually with major life experiences.

## The Big Five Personality Model

The Big Five (also called the Five-Factor Model) is the most widely validated framework in personality psychology. It describes personality along five broad dimensions, each representing a continuum from low to high.

### Openness to Experience

Openness reflects curiosity, creativity, and a preference for variety and novelty. High scorers tend to be imaginative, artistically sensitive, and intellectually curious. They enjoy exploring new ideas, are comfortable with abstraction, and often have broad interests.

Low scorers tend to prefer familiar routines, concrete thinking, and practical over theoretical matters. They are often described as conventional, down-to-earth, and focused.

### Conscientiousness

Conscientiousness reflects self-discipline, goal-directedness, and a tendency to be organised and dependable. High scorers are thorough, reliable, and persistent. They plan ahead, follow through on commitments, and tend to achieve at higher levels in structured environments.

Low scorers are more spontaneous, flexible, and comfortable with ambiguity. They may struggle with deadlines but thrive in situations requiring adaptability.

### Extraversion

Extraversion reflects the degree to which you draw energy from social interaction and external stimulation. High scorers are outgoing, assertive, and energised by being around others. They tend to experience more positive emotions and seek out excitement.

Low scorers (introverts) prefer solitude or small groups, think before speaking, and find large social gatherings draining. Introversion is not shyness — it reflects energy management, not social anxiety.

### Agreeableness

Agreeableness reflects your orientation toward others — cooperative, trusting, and empathic versus competitive, sceptical, and challenging. High scorers prioritise harmony, are quick to forgive, and tend to be altruistic.

Low scorers are more direct, willing to confront, and prioritise their own interests. They are often effective in competitive or negotiation-heavy contexts.

### Neuroticism (Emotional Stability)

Neuroticism measures the tendency to experience negative emotions such as anxiety, anger, and sadness. High scorers react more intensely to stress and perceived threats. They are often more vigilant and sensitive, which can be adaptive in dangerous environments.

Low scorers are emotionally stable — calm, resilient, and less reactive to setbacks. Emotional stability is associated with overall wellbeing and effective coping.

## How Scores Are Calculated

Each trait is scored on a 0–50 scale, where 25 represents the population average. Scores are derived from multiple survey items and then compared to a reference population of thousands of respondents.

Your percentile shows how your score compares to this reference group. A percentile of 70 on Conscientiousness means you scored higher than 70% of the reference respondents on that trait.

## Score Bands

Scores are grouped into three bands to simplify interpretation:

- **High** (score ≥ 31): Your score is clearly above average for this trait.
- **Middle** (score 20–30): Your score is near the population average.
- **Low** (score ≤ 19): Your score is clearly below average for this trait.

Neither high nor low scores are inherently better. Each profile has strengths and challenges depending on context.

## What Scores Are Not

Personality scores are not fixed diagnoses. They describe tendencies, not certainties. A high Neuroticism score means you tend to experience stress more intensely — it does not mean you cannot cope well or that you have a mental health condition.

Scores can also vary slightly depending on your current life circumstances. If you took the survey during an unusually stressful period, your Neuroticism score may be higher than usual.

## NEO Framework

The NEO framework maps scores onto the classic NEO Personality Inventory domains. It provides a clean five-factor view of your personality and is one of the most widely cited frameworks in the research literature.

## Facet-Level Scores

Within each Big Five domain, the survey measures narrower facets. For example, within Extraversion you may see scores for Warmth, Gregariousness, Assertiveness, Activity, Excitement Seeking, and Positive Emotions. Facet scores help explain patterns within a broader trait.

## Interpreting Your Profile as a Whole

Traits interact with each other. For example:
- High Openness + High Conscientiousness often produces effective, innovative professionals.
- High Agreeableness + Low Assertiveness (an Extraversion facet) may mean you avoid conflict even when speaking up would serve you well.
- High Neuroticism + Low Conscientiousness can create a pattern of stress without effective coping strategies.

Look for combinations, not just isolated high or low scores.
